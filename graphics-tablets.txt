Литературна компилация от полезна информация за графични таблети (таб

графични таблети - графични таблети за дигитално (цифрово) рисуване, като активната площ за рисуване е или непрозрачна не-LCD-екран зона или (при по-яките модели) е LCD-екран

марка&конкретен модел, година на производство, работоспособност/състояние (ново, използвано (работи добре), използвано (работи, но с някои проблеми/дефекти/ограничения), използвано (не работи), използвано (за продан/продажба на частите)...)

чувствителност/прецизност:
* брой нива на натиск/допир
* (брой нива на) наклон (на писалката)
* (брой нива на) завъртане (на писалката)
* брой и тип и функции на писалката и на перата/върховете ѝ (например отделен eID за софтуерна персонализация на всяка и използване на няколко писалки (stylus) едновременно; "гума за триене" на обратната страна на писалката, с опция за софтуерна настройка/преконфигуриране в друг вид връх на писалката, с други софтуерни художествени четки вътре в компютърната програма за рисуване и редактиране на изображения и за ретуширане на снимки...)
* touchscreen=graphics tablet drawing active/passive area VS. no touchscreen=separate computer display/monitor from the graphics tablet active/passive drawing area...
* допълнителни бързи хардуерни бутони, които може да се настройват софтуерно каква функция да изпълняват (например като комбинация от клавиши на компютърната клавиатура или детен бутон на мишката или комбинации от бутоните на компютърната мишка/laptop touchpad/laptop pointing ThinkPad joystick controller)...
* здравина&стил/красота/естетика на тялото на графичния таблет и здрава кутия/раница за гърба със защитно отделение за лаптопи, твърди дискове и т.н.
* надраскване на площта на повърхността за рисуване на графичния таблет и опции за смяната на тази повърхност за рисуване и опции за защитата ѝ със защитно фолио или прозрачен пластмасов класьор (?!?)...
* конкретни компютърни програми — софтуер за рисуване и редактиране на изображения
* USB кабел за свързване (здравина, дебелина, USBv1/2/3+?, ел. захранващо USB; Lightning connector; DisplayPort/USB3+); дали USB кабелът е свързан неподвижно към таблета или може да се откача и от двата края
* хардуерни светлинни&звукови показатели (индикатори) върху таблета и софтуерната им настройка
* на коя OS (операционна система), нейна версия и с кои софтуерни драйвери може да се използва графичният таблет за рисуване (wacom драйвер за Linux (версия и т.н.), DIGImend драйвера за не-wacom графични таблети; допълнителен софтуер за настройка на wacom таблети; фабричен софтуер за настройка на Wacom таблети; wintab драйвера в MS Windows vs. (срещу) по-новия драйвер за графични таблети в Windows10...

Drawing Software

There is a lot of drawing software. The list here is only just scratching the surface. These are the apps that I like to use.

￼

Adobe Creative Cloud

Creative Cloud includes a LOT. Photoshop, Illustrator, Animator, and much more beyond illustration and drawing. It's also widely considered the industry standard for graphics of all kinds. The catch? It costs and arm and a leg and it also has a much steeper learning curve if you're coming in cold.

Price: $50 a monthLEARN MORE

￼

Clip Studio

I've moved Clip Studio up this list because there is now an iPad app. The desktop app has been the favorite for many a illustrator for years and the iPad app is a a feature for feature replication of the desktop app. The one downside is that the iPad app has an $8.99 a month subscription attached to it. That's a little surprising since you can usually find the desktop app on sale for less than $50.

Price: $50LEARN MORE

￼

Sketchable

Sketchable is a Windows only drawing app and the reason I like it is because of its simplicity. It doesn't do as much as some of the other drawing and painting apps out there but it's so elegant in its simplicity. It's also super smooth, pinching to zoom in and out on a touch screen is great and it really feels like it's designed for tablet computers.

table-price: Free with in app purchasesLEARN MORE

￼

Affinity Designer

The more I use Affinity Designer the more I like it. It combines the vector tools of Adobe Illustrator with many of the painting features of Adobe Photoshop. It's a pro grade for a not so Pro table-pricetag.

Price: $50LEARN MORE

￼

Procreate

If you have an iPad you need Procreate. Don't let the low table-price fool you, this is an amazing little painting app. This is the app that made me love drawing on the iPad. So simple and elegant but also deep and powerful.

Price: $10LEARN MORE

￼

Krita

Krita has really grown on me and it's free. It's one of the most robust free drawing apps availible on Windows, Mac and Linux.

Price: FreeLEARN MORE

￼

Autodesk Sketchbook

Sketchbook isn't my favorite program to draw in but I like that it's cross platform. If I draw something on Android I can open it up on my Mac or iPad. The interface is also easy to understand and pick up.

Price: FreeLEARN MORE

￼

Medibang Paint

Medibang reminds me of Clip studio with less stuff. Considering it's free it's an impressively robust drawing and painting app.

Price: FreeLEARN MORE

Drawing Software

There is a lot of drawing software. The list here is only just scratching the surface. These are the apps that I like to use.

￼

Adobe Creative Cloud

Creative Cloud includes a LOT. Photoshop, Illustrator, Animator, and much more beyond illustration and drawing. It's also widely considered the industry standard for graphics of all kinds. The catch? It costs and arm and a leg and it also has a much steeper learning curve if you're coming in cold.

Price: $50 a monthLEARN MORE

￼

Clip Studio

I've moved Clip Studio up this list because there is now an iPad app. The desktop app has been the favorite for many a illustrator for years and the iPad app is a a feature for feature replication of the desktop app. The one downside is that the iPad app has an $8.99 a month subscription attached to it. That's a little surprising since you can usually find the desktop app on sale for less than $50.

Price: $50LEARN MORE

￼

Sketchable

Sketchable is a Windows only drawing app and the reason I like it is because of its simplicity. It doesn't do as much as some of the other drawing and painting apps out there but it's so elegant in its simplicity. It's also super smooth, pinching to zoom in and out on a touch screen is great and it really feels like it's designed for tablet computers.

table-price: Free with in app purchasesLEARN MORE

￼

Affinity Designer

The more I use Affinity Designer the more I like it. It combines the vector tools of Adobe Illustrator with many of the painting features of Adobe Photoshop. It's a pro grade for a not so Pro table-pricetag.

Price: $50LEARN MORE

￼

Procreate

If you have an iPad you need Procreate. Don't let the low table-price fool you, this is an amazing little painting app. This is the app that made me love drawing on the iPad. So simple and elegant but also deep and powerful.

Price: $10LEARN MORE

￼

Krita

Krita has really grown on me and it's free. It's one of the most robust free drawing apps availible on Windows, Mac and Linux.

Price: FreeLEARN MORE

￼

Autodesk Sketchbook

Sketchbook isn't my favorite program to draw in but I like that it's cross platform. If I draw something on Android I can open it up on my Mac or iPad. The interface is also easy to understand and pick up.

Price: FreeLEARN MORE

￼

Medibang Paint

Medibang reminds me of Clip studio with less stuff. Considering it's free it's an impressively robust drawing and painting app.

Price: FreeLEARN MORE
