A painting hangs upon the wall, depicting the view from inside a dimly-lit room to the sunlit garden beyond. Although the painting is very well executed, you can't see much artistic merit in it - until suddenly you realise that the doorway in the painting is the doorway of the library, and the garden is the island you've been exploring.

Why would someone hang a picture of a doorway in a room that contains the very same thing?
