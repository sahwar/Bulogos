Things already seen¶

Most of these I’ve seen without reference to the above list. I’ll include a comment on each one so you can get an idea of what movies I like.

  • Screwtape needs to see Fight Club
    • Not too violent, and deliciously mind-messing-with.
  • Screwtape needs to read Snow Crash
    • The start of my lengthy Neal Stephenson fandom.
  • Screwtape needs to see Being John Malkovich
    • Poignant, hilarious, and deliciously brain-twisting.
  • Screwtape needs to see Blade Runner
    • I appreciated all the effort put into evoking an atmosphere; I kind of wish I’d seen the version with explanatory hard-boiled voice-over.
  • Screwtape needs to see Spaceballs
    • If it was ever funny, it hasn’t aged well.
  • Screwtape needs to see the composer commentary on The Matrix DVD. it’s very good.
    • The composer was interesting, I found the backing music incredibly irritating.
  • Screwtape needs to see 12 monkeys
    • A bit depressing, but well-plotted.
  • Screwtape needs to see Life of Brian
    • As usual with the Pythons, a few really great scenes sandwiched between less-great scenes, and scenes that might have been funnier if I hadn’t been hearing them quoted verbatim by fans for twenty years.
  • Screwtape needs to watch This Is Spinal Tap
    • Kept me amused, but I’m more pleased the fact I can say I’ve seen it than I was by watching it.
  • Screwtape needs to see Pirates of the Carribean.
    • Great fun, but deep down I wished I were watching The Secret Of Monkey Island: The Movie instead.
  • Screwtape needs to see Bambi vs Godzilla
    • I laughed. Twice.
  • tell Screwtape to see Inception.
    • A beautifully-executed take on a very old idea, worth going out of your way to see.
  • Screwtape needs to see both Bill and Ted movies
    • Enjoyable but mindless, probably not worth going out of your way to see.
  • Screwtape = needs to watch Monkey!
    • I was very impressed at how many plotlines they had that I’d never seen anywhere else.
  • Screwtape: you should see Anchorman.
    • This was terrible. Even the (very) few jokes that started out funny kept going until they stopped being funny and started being hair-tearingly frustrating.
