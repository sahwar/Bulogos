A promenade stretches along the edge of the island here. Above the dock, but below the summit of the island, it offers a magnificent view over the ocean - which would be more interesting if there were a single visual feature more than a few yards offshore. Out from the lee of the island, you can feel a gentle sea-breeze ruffling your hair.

At one end of the promenade, a small set of stairs will take you down to the dock, or up to some kind of mechanically-obsessed sculpture. At the other end, a series of logs forms a pathway up to the rest of the island - the closest feature is a little dome-roofed circular structure made from granite, complete with carved columns all the way around. Beyond that you can see a squarish sort of structure that also has stone columns, while in other directions you can see a forest, and an enormous rocky spire with a huge metallic cylinder on top.

To your surprise, you see a piece of paper fluttering about in the breeze.

You may:

Wander over to the stairs,

Climb up to the giant gears,

Enter the domed Planetarium,

Pick up the paper or

Follow the log path.
